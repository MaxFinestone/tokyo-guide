Street Food · Tsukiji

Tsukiji Outer Market

Tsukiji, Chūō  ·  Open from 5 am  ·  By Maxence Finestone

Home› Eat & Drink› Tsukiji Outer Market

Tokyo's most famous food market is alive and well — the outer stalls still serve the freshest tamagoyaki, sea urchin, grilled scallops, and sushi you'll find anywhere in the city.

When the inner wholesale market relocated to Toyosu in 2018, many feared Tsukiji was finished. They were wrong. The outer market — the rows of retail stalls and street food vendors that surround the old wholesale hall — remained, and if anything became even more of a destination. Arrive before 9 am to see it at its best.

What to Eat

The outer market's speciality is immediate, standing food: fresh tamagoyaki (thick sweet egg omelette cooked to order), sea urchin (uni) served directly from the shell, giant grilled scallops basted in soy butter, and fresh-cut tuna sashimi sold from polystyrene trays with wooden chopsticks. Many stalls also sell sushi at surprisingly reasonable prices given the quality.

What to Buy

Beyond food, Tsukiji is famous for professional kitchen knives — the small knife shops here sell blades used by Tokyo's top chefs. Dried seaweed, pickles, condiments, and cooking equipment round out the shopping options.

  • Arrive before 9 am — the best stalls sell out early and crowds build quickly.
  • Most transactions are cash only; bring yen.
  • The market is closed on Sundays and some Wednesdays.
  • Combine with a walk to Hamarikyu Gardens nearby.

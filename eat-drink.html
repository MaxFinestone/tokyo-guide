Tokyo Dining

Eat & Drink

From Michelin-starred omakase counters to cat cafés — Tokyo feeds every conceivable appetite.

Ramen & Noodles

Tokyo's ramen culture is unmatched — every bowl is a statement.

Ramen · Nationwide

Ichiran

Tokyo's most famous solo ramen experience. Each diner sits in a private booth, ordering via paper form. Rich tonkotsu broth, perfectly calibrated to your taste.

¥890–¥1,500 · No reservationRead more →
Tsukemen · Shinjuku

Fu-unji

Legendary dipping ramen in Shinjuku. Thick wavy noodles are dunked into an intensely rich fish-and-pork broth. Queues form before opening — arrive early.

¥900–¥1,200 · Cash onlyShinjuku-ku

Sushi & Seafood

From ¥100 conveyor belts to Michelin counters — Tokyo has every tier.

Sushi · Tsukiji⭐ Michelin

Matsunozushi

A Michelin-starred omakase counter near Tsukiji. Freshness that will permanently raise your expectations for sushi. Reserve weeks in advance.

¥15,000–30,000 · Reservation requiredRead more →
Kaiten-zushi · Citywide

Kura Sushi

Japan's best conveyor-belt sushi chain. Order via touchscreen, watch plates arrive on the belt. Every 5 plates enters you into a prize game. Brilliant fun at ¥110/plate.

¥110–¥330 per plate · No reservationMultiple locations
Market · Chuo

Tsukiji Outer Market

The famous fish market's outer ring is still buzzing with street food stalls. Tamagoyaki on a stick, fresh uni, grilled scallops, and some of the city's best tuna rolls.

¥200–¥2,000 · Best 7–10amRead more →

Meat & Grill

Yakitori alleys, tonkatsu temples, and premium wagyu.

Yakitori · Yurakucho

Under the Tracks

The yakitori stalls crammed beneath Yurakucho station's elevated tracks are one of Tokyo's great atmospheric eating experiences. Smoke, beer, and skewers — perfect after work.

¥150–¥400 per skewer · Evenings onlyYurakucho Station
Tonkatsu · Omotesando

Maisen

Tokyo's most celebrated tonkatsu, housed in a converted bathhouse in Omotesando. The kurobuta (black pig) katsu is absurdly tender. Queue on weekends — it's worth it.

¥1,800–¥3,500 · Weekday lunch bestOmotesando
Gyoza · Harajuku

Harajuku Gyozaro

Tiny standing gyoza bar that has been serving crispy-bottomed pan-fried dumplings since 1952. Six gyoza and a beer — simple, cheap, and absolutely perfect. Always a queue.

¥270 for 6 pcs · Cash onlyHarajuku
Tempura · Ginza⭐ Michelin

Tempura Kondo

Two-Michelin-star tempura in Ginza. Kondo-san is famous for his vegetable tempura — a whole sweet potato fried over 16 minutes. High-end but worth the special occasion spend.

¥15,000+ · Reservation essentialGinza 5-chome

Cafés & Sweet Things

Tokyo's café culture rivals any city on earth.

Café · Multiple Locations

Cat Cafe Mocha

Spend an hour with a dozen friendly cats at one of Tokyo's most beloved cat cafés. The perfect antidote to a long day of temple-hopping.

¥800 entry · drink includedRead more →
Matcha · Ginza / Omotesando

Nakamura Tokichi

A 160-year-old Uji tea house with a Tokyo outpost. The matcha parfait — layered with warabi mochi, red bean, and green tea ice cream — is one of the city's most Instagrammed desserts.

¥1,300–¥2,000 · Queues at weekendsOmotesando Hills
Street Food · Harajuku

Takeshita Street Crepes

Tokyo-style crepes are a different beast — rolled into a cone and stuffed with whipped cream, strawberries, and custard. Marion Crepes has been perfecting them on Takeshita Dori since 1976.

¥400–¥800 · Walk and eatTakeshita Dori, Harajuku

Izakaya & Drinks

Japan's pub culture at its best — order everything, share everything.

Izakaya · Citywide

Torikizoku

The best cheap izakaya chain in Japan — every single item on the menu costs ¥298. Yakitori, edamame, karaage, cold Sapporo — an entire evening costs barely ¥2,000. Unmissable.

¥298 per dish · Evening onlyMultiple locations
Bar · Shinjuku

Golden Gai

A labyrinth of 200 tiny bars, each seating six to eight people, in the backstreets of Shinjuku. Some serve only regulars; most welcome strangers with curiosity. Pure Tokyo.

¥500–¥1,000 seating charge typicalKabukicho, Shinjuku

Don't Miss

Experiences no visitor should leave Tokyo without.

Food Hall · Shinjuku / Ginza

Depachika

The basement food floors of department stores like Isetan (Shinjuku) or Mitsukoshi (Ginza) are a world unto themselves: wagashi, prepared bento, premium fruit, and endless tastings.

¥300–¥3,000 · Open daily till 8pmIsetan B1–B2, Shinjuku
Convenience Store · Everywhere

7-Eleven & FamilyMart

Japanese convenience stores are a food experience in their own right. Onigiri (¥120), hot karaage, sandwiches that shame most cafés, and seasonal limited editions. Eat like a local.

¥100–¥600 · Open 24 hoursEvery corner of the city

Hidden Gems

Beloved by locals, overlooked by most tourists — these are the spots worth seeking out.

Ramen · Nakameguro

Afuri

Yuzu shio ramen — a light, fragrant broth unlike the heavy tonkotsu most tourists try first. The citrus notes are subtle but unmistakeable. Minimalist interior, maximum flavour.

¥1,100–¥1,400 · No reservationNakameguro / Ebisu
Vegan Ramen · Tokyo Station

T's Tantan

Fully vegan sesame tantanmen hidden on Keiyo Street B1 inside Tokyo Station. A revelation for vegetarians who assumed Tokyo was off-limits. Rich broth, zero compromise.

¥900–¥1,100 · No reservationTokyo Station, Keiyo St B1
Sushi · Shibuya

Midori Sushi

The best affordable sushi secret among Tokyo locals. Lines form daily at the Shibuya Hikarie branch — arrive before opening or accept a 45-minute wait. Exceptionally fresh nigiri at prices that shame the competition.

¥200–¥500 per plate · No reservationShibuya Hikarie
Soba · Kanda

Kanda Matsuya

Open since 1884 and still handmaking soba the same way. The old timber building near Akihabara feels untouched by time. No English menu — point at the duck seiro and leave happy.

¥900–¥1,800 · Lunch onlyKanda, Chiyoda-ku
Street Food · Yanaka

Yanaka Ginza

A covered shopping street that survived the war and refuses to modernise. Takoyaki, freshly fried menchi katsu, tamagoyaki on a stick, and melon pan. The most atmospheric street-food walk in old Tokyo.

¥100–¥600 · Afternoons bestYanaka, Taito-ku
Okonomiyaki · Asakusa

Sometaro

Cook your own okonomiyaki on a cast-iron teppan grill in a century-old machiya house, steps from Senso-ji. Tatami seating, paper lanterns, and the smell of pork and cabbage sizzling. Probably the most atmospheric meal in Asakusa.

¥1,200–¥1,800 · Walk-ins welcomeNishi-Asakusa
Coffee · Tomigaya

Fuglen Tokyo

A Norwegian café transplant that became one of Tokyo's finest specialty coffee spots. By evening it quietly pivots to a cocktail bar. The Tomigaya neighbourhood around it is worth an entire afternoon of wandering.

Coffee ¥600–¥900 · Cocktails ¥1,200+Tomigaya, Shibuya
Izakaya · Ebisu

Ebisu Yokocho

A covered alley of 20 tiny izakayas — more local and far less touristy than Golden Gai. Order grilled fish, oden, and cold Sapporo while sitting elbow-to-elbow with regulars who've had the same stool for years.

¥500–¥2,000 · Evenings onlyEbisu, Shibuya-ku
Whisky Bar · Ginza

Bar High Five

Run by Hidetsugu Ueno, widely regarded as one of the world's great bartenders. A subterranean Ginza bar with an extraordinary Japanese whisky selection. Worth every yen for a special-occasion drink.

¥2,000–¥4,000 per drink · Reservation recommendedGinza 5-chome

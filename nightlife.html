Tokyo Nightlife

Shinjuku & Shibuya  ·  After Dark Guide  ·  By Maxence Finestone

Home› Nightlife

From world-class electronic clubs to six-seat whisky bars tucked down Shinjuku alleys — Tokyo's night scene is as vast and varied as the city itself. The city doesn't sleep.

Tokyo is one of the world's great after-dark cities. Unlike many capitals, its nightlife is spread across dozens of distinct micro-scenes — from the sprawling megaclubs of Shibuya to the quiet jazz bars of Shinjuku's Golden Gai, each pocket offering something entirely its own.

Shibuya — Electronic & Megaclubs

Shibuya is Tokyo's undisputed club capital. WOMB on Maruyamacho is the flagship — a four-floor club with a legendary main room sound system that has hosted every major electronic DJ in the world. Club Asia and Atom Tokyo nearby keep the neighbourhood busy until sunrise on weekends. The streets of Center-gai hum with izakayas and karaoke parlours for when the clubs close.

Shinjuku — Golden Gai & Kabukichō

Golden Gai is one of Tokyo's most extraordinary places: a grid of six narrow alleys holding roughly 200 tiny bars, each seating between four and eight people. Every bar has its own personality — jazz, film, punk, literature. Regulars are fiercely loyal; tourists are welcomed. Arrive after 10 pm and wander until you find one that feels right.

Kabukichō is Tokyo's biggest entertainment district — pachinko parlours, host clubs, karaoke towers, and ramen shops that open at 2 am. It's relentless and fascinating. The new Kabukicho Tower brought cinemas, restaurants, and a theatre to the area.

Roppongi — International & Late Night

Roppongi has long been Tokyo's international nightlife hub. Muse, V2 Tokyo, and Odeon are reliable multifloor clubs. The neighbourhood stays lively until the trains resume at 5 am and serves a more international crowd than Shibuya or Shinjuku.

Practical Tips

  • Tokyo's trains stop around midnight and resume at 5 am — plan accordingly or budget for a taxi.
  • Most clubs charge ¥2,000–3,500 entry including one or two drinks.
  • Golden Gai bars typically charge a small seating fee (¥500–800) on top of drinks.
  • Dress code at major clubs is smart casual — trainers are usually fine, but flip-flops are not.
  • Konbini (convenience stores) are lifesavers at 3 am — hot food, drinks, and ATMs all night.

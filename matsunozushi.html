Michelin-Starred Sushi · Tsukiji

Matsunozushi

Near Tsukiji Market  ·  5 min read  ·  By Maxence Finestone

Home›Attractions›Matsunozushi

A Michelin-starred omakase counter where the freshness of the fish will ruin supermarket sushi for you permanently. Reserve well in advance.

Tokyo has more Michelin-starred restaurants than any other city on earth, and its sushi counters occupy a rarefied category within that firmament. Matsunozushi is a testament to what Japanese cuisine achieves when tradition, restraint, and extraordinary ingredients converge.

The Omakase

An omakase meal is an act of trust: you sit at the counter and eat whatever the itamae (chef) presents. Each nigiri piece arrives directly on the lacquered counter — wasabi already folded in, rice temperature matching your body heat, fish sourced this morning.

What to Expect

A typical course runs 12–20 pieces across roughly 90 minutes: otoro (fatty bluefin), uni (sea urchin), kohada (cured gizzard shad), anago (sea eel), and whatever the market yielded that morning.

  • Reserve 1–2 weeks ahead; use Pocket Concierge or Tableall.
  • Budget ¥15,000–30,000 per person.
  • Dress smart-casual.
  • Inform the restaurant of dietary restrictions well in advance.

Landmark · Shibuya

Shibuya Scramble

Shibuya, Tokyo  ·  4 min read  ·  By Maxence Finestone

Home›Attractions›Shibuya Scramble

When every light turns red simultaneously, up to 3,000 people flood the intersection at once — pure, distilled Tokyo energy.

The Shibuya Scramble Crossing is the world's most photographed intersection. Every two minutes, all vehicles stop and pedestrians flood from every direction in a perfectly orderly swarm.

Best Vantage Points

At street level, crossing with the crowd is exhilarating. For the iconic overhead shot: Starbucks second floor (northwest corner, opens 7am). Mag's Park rooftop on Scramble Square offers the ultimate bird's-eye view from 47 metres.

When to Visit

Friday and Saturday evenings are most electric — 4,000+ people on a single crossing has been recorded. Rain intensifies the spectacle: a forest of umbrellas creates a kaleidoscopic effect from above.

  • Friday evenings 6–9pm for maximum crowds.
  • Starbucks window seats fill fast — arrive at opening time.
  • The Hachiko dog statue outside Shibuya Station is the classic meeting point.
  • The crossing is free — it's a public road.

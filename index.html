Cultural Guide

Respect & Etiquette

Japan's culture rewards those who arrive prepared. A few customs to know before you land.

Remove shoes at temples & homes

Look for the step at an entrance (genkan) and the row of shoes. Follow suit — this is non-negotiable in Japanese homes and many traditional spaces.

Quiet on the train

Tokyo's trains are remarkably silent. Keep phone calls to a whisper, mute your device, and avoid eating. You'll fit right in.

Cash is still king

Many restaurants, shrines, and smaller shops are cash-only. Carry yen. 7-Eleven and Japan Post ATMs reliably accept foreign cards.

Bow as a greeting

A small bow goes a long way. You don't need to perfect the angle — any sincere effort is appreciated and will be returned with warmth.

Also Worth Exploring

🤼

Ryōgoku Kokugikan

Japan's national sumo arena, where the ancient sport of sumo has been contested for centuries. Tournament season tickets sell out fast.

🎭

Ariake Arena

Tokyo's premier modern venue for concerts and major events. Built for the 2020 Olympics, now a cultural hotspot in the waterfront district.

🐼

Ueno Zoo

Japan's oldest zoo, home to over 3,000 animals across 350 species — including the city's beloved giant pandas. A favourite with families.

🎰

Vending Machines

Tokyo has one vending machine for every 23 residents. Hot canned coffee, fresh ramen, umbrellas — a quirky cultural institution.

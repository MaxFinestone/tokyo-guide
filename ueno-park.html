Park · Ueno

Ueno Park

Ueno, Taito City  ·  5 min read  ·  By Maxence Finestone

Home›Attractions›Ueno Park

Tokyo's most culturally dense park: world-class museums, Japan's oldest zoo, a lotus lake, and 1,000 cherry trees in a single square kilometre.

Ueno Park is Tokyo's cultural epicentre — 53 hectares of museums, temples, a shrine, Japan's oldest zoo, a lotus-covered lake, and spring cherry blossoms that draw a million visitors in a single weekend.

Museum Row

The park's north edge holds Tokyo National Museum (110,000 objects), Tokyo Metropolitan Art Museum, National Museum of Nature and Science, and the National Museum of Western Art — whose permanent collection includes Monet, Renoir, Picasso, and a world-famous Rodin sculpture garden.

Shinobazu Pond

The park's centrepiece: a pond split into a boating area, nature reserve, and Benten-do island temple. In summer, lotus flowers carpet the water entirely in pink and white.

  • The park itself is free; museums and zoo charge separately (zoo ¥600 adults).
  • Weekday mornings are quietest for the museums.
  • Combine with Yanaka neighbourhood for old Tokyo atmosphere.
  • The sakura promenade runs north-south through the park.

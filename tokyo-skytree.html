Tower · Sumida

Tokyo Skytree

Oshiage, Sumida  ·  5 min read  ·  By Maxence Finestone

Home›Attractions›Tokyo Skytree

At 634 metres, Japan's tallest structure offers views across the entire Kanto plain — on clear winter mornings, all the way to Mount Fuji.

Completed in 2012, Tokyo Skytree stands at 634 metres — a height chosen deliberately. The digits 6-3-4 read as mu-sa-shi in Japanese, an ancient name for the Tokyo region.

Two Observation Decks

The Tembo Deck at 350m offers 360° floor-to-ceiling glass panoramas. On clear days, visibility extends 70+ km encompassing Mount Fuji and the Pacific coast. The Tembo Galleria at 450m is a spiralling glass walkway with transparent floor sections not for the faint-hearted.

Tokyo Skytree Town

The base houses Solamachi: 300+ shops, the Sumida Aquarium, a planetarium, and an entire floor dedicated to Japanese regional food souvenirs. Budget a full afternoon for both tower and base.

  • Pre-book tickets online to skip queues (walk-up waits can exceed 90 minutes on weekends).
  • The Skytree Passport combines both decks at a discount.
  • Best exterior photographs from the Oshiage canal 400m south.
  • Visit on a clear winter morning for Mount Fuji views.

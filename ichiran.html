Ichiran Ramen

Multiple locations  ·  ¥900–1,200 per bowl  ·  By Maxence Finestone

Home› Eat & Drink› Ichiran Ramen

Japan's most famous solo-dining ramen experience — a personal booth, a bamboo curtain, and a bowl of tonkotsu broth dialled to your exact specification.

Ichiran is unlike any other ramen restaurant. You enter, purchase your ticket from a vending machine, and are guided to a private wooden booth separated from neighbours by dividers. A short form allows you to customise every element: richness of broth, firmness of noodles, amount of garlic, spice level, and more.

The Experience

Once seated, you slide your order form under the bamboo curtain. It rises briefly to deliver your bowl and then closes again. The entire meal is conducted in near-silence — no distractions, no social obligation, just you and the ramen. It is a uniquely Japanese approach to dining: deeply personal, efficient, and entirely focused on the food.

The Ramen

Ichiran's tonkotsu broth is its signature — milky, rich, and built on a pork bone base. The secret sauce (tare) is Ichiran's most closely guarded recipe. Noodles are thin and springy. The chashu pork melts. You will almost certainly order a second helping (kaedama — replacement noodles for the remaining broth).

  • Open 24 hours at most locations — perfect for late-night hunger.
  • No reservations required; queue at the door.
  • Shibuya, Shinjuku, Harajuku, Asakusa, and airport locations all available.
  • Vegetarian option available at select branches.

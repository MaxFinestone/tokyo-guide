Temple · Asakusa

Sensō-ji Temple

Asakusa, Tokyo  ·  6 min read  ·  By Maxence Finestone

Home›Attractions›Sensō-ji Temple

Tokyo's oldest and most cherished temple — where centuries of devotion, incense smoke, and millions of footsteps converge beneath the iconic Kaminarimon Thunder Gate.

Founded in 628 AD, Sensō-ji is Tokyo's oldest temple, dedicated to Kannon, the Buddhist goddess of mercy, and draws over 30 million visitors annually.

The Kaminarimon Thunder Gate

The towering Kaminarimon (Thunder Gate) greets you before the temple itself. Its enormous red paper lantern — nearly 700 kg — is one of Tokyo's most photographed sights. Beyond it runs Nakamise-dori, 250 metres of traditional stalls selling ningyo-yaki cakes, ningyo dolls, and handcrafted souvenirs.

The Main Hall

The five-storey pagoda and main hall anchor the complex. Worshippers shake canisters to receive fortune sticks (omikuji) and hang wooden wishing plaques (ema) inscribed with prayers. The massive bronze cauldron billows incense smoke — rubbing it on your body is said to heal ailments.

When to Visit

Early morning before 8am is transformative: sparse crowds, serene atmosphere, beautiful lighting. The grounds are open 24 hours. The Sanja Matsuri festival in May is one of Tokyo's grandest celebrations.

  • Admission is free; grounds open around the clock.
  • Best photo of the Thunder Gate: stand back on the main street, shoot straight toward the temple.
  • Rickshaw (jinrikisha) tours of Asakusa depart just outside.
  • Combine with a walk to Nakamise-dori for traditional snacks.

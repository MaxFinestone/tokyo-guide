Neighborhood Guide

Ginza

Tokyo's most refined district — luxury flagships, art galleries, and some of the finest restaurants in the world

Home›Neighborhoods›Ginza

Ginza is Tokyo at its most polished. A precise grid of boulevards holds flagship stores for every luxury brand on earth alongside cutting-edge Japanese concept shops, galleries, and restaurants where a lunch reservation requires weeks of planning.

The name means “silver mint” — silver coins were produced here in the Edo period. Chuo-dori, the main boulevard, closes to traffic on weekend afternoons, becoming a promenade where Tokyoites stroll unhurriedly.

What to See

Itoya — Twelve floors of the world's most beautiful stationery. Even if you never write by hand, you'll want a pen. Mitsukoshi — The great Tokyo department store (founded 1673); the basement food hall is extraordinary. Kabuki-za Theatre — Japan's premier kabuki theatre; single-act tickets available.
